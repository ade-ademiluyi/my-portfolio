Adetowo Ademiluyi was born on 20 July, 1999 in Lagos, Nigeria, where he lived
all his life until he went to college in the United States. He is the
first and only male child in a family of four. His father, a retired civil
servant, and his mother, an auxiliary nurse, are both Christians, and are involved heavily in the
community and in the church. He and his family lived a rural life with similar
backgrounds, and intimate familiar ties with other members of the community. In his community, not
everyone has the privilege of having an education, and as a result, several of the
women get married soon after hitting puberty and become stay at home moms who take care
of the children and house work that has to be done. Not all of the men
attend college either, most of them choose to learn a trade as soon as they
can and settle down early with a family. Some make the choice of taking jobs as
factory workers as an alternative to attending college. These varieties of lifestyle are somewhat
popular where he comes from, and growing up in that community has probably had the
most impact on the person that he is today.



Adetowo comes from a very small community where everybody is pretty much the same, there's
not much diversity in the community as majority of the people are blacks and the
few whites in the community are usually there for tourism. Not having an education was the
norm, and even though he was lucky to receive an education comparable to the united states
high school diploma, he knew he wanted more from life. Growing up, his mother’s version of
Christianity included a heavy dose of social responsibility. This pushed him earlier into social work and
education, but being a bit rebellious by nature, He took his own path getting there. He
was influenced on a Cultural level by a history he lived through as a child: getting an
education wasn’t seen as a necessity, but as something only rich people could have. He
knew the world could be a harsh place and believed that he could have a role
in making it better through education and he worked hard to achieve that. Consequently, he was
able to secure a scholarship to get his bachelor’s degree in the United States and fulfill
his dream of getting an education.



Even though he lived most of his life in Nigeria where blacks are the majority,
there was still diversity as there are different people from different cultures and his life
experiences opened him up to accepting and appreciating those who are culturally diverse from him. while
his ability to form relationships with diverse individuals has made him more appreciative of other
races, he understands that this does not mean that he knows what life is like as a
minority. He has lived his whole life comfortably as part of the majority group in Nigeria
.
